#  Twitch Channel Performance & Viewer Engagement Analysis (SQL)
Rhoda Salome Daniel
April 2026

##  Project Overview
This project analyzes Twitch channel performance data using SQL to uncover insights about viewer engagement, content popularity, and follower growth efficiency.

The goal is to understand:
- What drives viewer engagement?
- Which channels convert viewers into followers most effectively?
- How esports events compare to individual streamers?

---

##  Dataset Description
The dataset contains Twitch channel-level metrics including:
- Watch time
- Stream time
- Average viewers
- Peak viewers
- Followers gained
- Views gained
- Language
- Partner status

---

##  Key Business Questions
- Which channels generate the highest engagement?
- Do esports events distort engagement metrics?
- Does high engagement lead to follower growth?
- Which channels convert viewers into followers most efficiently?

---

##  Tools Used
- SQL (SQL Server Management System)
- Data aggregation & filtering
- KPI creation

---

## Key Metrics Created

### 1. Engagement Ratio
Watch Time / Stream Time

### 2. Growth Efficiency
Followers Gained / Watch Time

---

##  Key Insights

### 1. Engagement is highly skewed by esports events
Channels like major tournament broadcasts dominate raw engagement metrics.

### 2. After filtering esports, engagement becomes more balanced
Individual streamers show a more even distribution of performance.

### 3. High engagement does not guarantee high follower growth
Top streamers are not always the most efficient in converting viewers into followers.

### 4. Smaller creators show higher growth efficiency
Niche creators often convert audience attention into followers more effectively.

### 5. The Twitch ecosystem has two distinct segments:
- High-volume esports/events
- Individual content creators

---

##  Final Takeaway
Viewer attention (engagement) and audience growth (conversion) behave as two separate business metrics. Optimizing one does not guarantee improvement in the other.
